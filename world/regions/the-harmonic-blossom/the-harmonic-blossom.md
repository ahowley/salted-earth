### Overview

The Harmonic Blossom is a sacred location on Uldura, being host to a freshwater
oasis (note: most people on Uldura do not have a word for an "oasis", and often
simply "blossom" is a more apt translation). This is the only large body of
water in the known world. The coasts are host to a sprawl of colorful wildlife,
flora, and fauna, with something akin to a rainforest bordering the desert in an
almost comically sharp barrier.

It is not widely known why the blossom formed or how it is maintained, but it is
the most common pilgramage made by almost every culture on Uldura. This trip has
a variety of names, but all center on bringing water back home - or "home," for
the mostly-nomadic people here.

There may be a hint in the name, and the waves, or lack thereof. There seems to
be some inaudible sound or vibration that emanates non-stop from the center of
the body of water (which is virtually flawlessly round), and a staunch lack of
wind give everything an eery stillness except the surface, which flutters
nonstop like frosted glass in tiny, almost unnoticable waves. You'd be forgiven
for thinking the water is foggy or murky, despite the fact it seems
supernaturally clean.

One might expect this place to be a bloodbath, constantly fighting over fresh
water. Unexpectedly, however, no one spills blood in the Blossom, including
wildlife. It is a peaceful place by its nature - whether this is a law of
society, a law of nature, or a simple coincidence, I leave this to you to
discover.

### A Note on Winter

While the above description is generally accurate, it was written prior to my
presence in the winter. The entire blossom freezes solid almost all winter -
which is short, but harsh - a period of time during which life seems to retreat
from the world entirely here. There's no decay, fallen plants simply get
swallowed by the sand in a matter of weeks.

This offers a possible explanation for a question I've had from my arrival: Why
don't people settle on the Blossom? The reason is simple - although a cradle of
life for most of the year, for a portion of it the harmony here dissipates into
one of the most hostile environments I have ever witness. The winds returns like
a rampaging beast eager for revenge.

At the wrong time of year, one could easily be misled to believe there is
nothing here but a frozen hole in the desert.
