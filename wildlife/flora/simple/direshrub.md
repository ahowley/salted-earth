## Overview

Technically a tree, this skeletal shrub has distinctive, blood red branches and
a trunk that only extends up to a half a meter from the ground

## Lifespan

One year

## Notes

These strange-looking plants have a mangled, almost violent appearance, but this
appears to be an adaptation to prevent them from being eaten. Surprisingly, this
seems to work, as very few creatures feed on Direshrubs, despite them seeming,
to me, to be perfectly edible in theory.

They have the interesting quality that they self-reproduce with genetically
identical copies of themselves, only once per season. Despite having few natural
"predators", they seem to only produce one or two offspring a year, meaning they
spread incredibly slowly, if at all.

Because their lifespan roughly matches their reproductive cycle, it is not
uncommon to find two identical shrubs adjacent to one an other - it is often
impossible to distinguish parent from child.
