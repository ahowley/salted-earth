## Notes

Bleachgrass is a very short, but very dense, grass that is often found growing
in the sand immediately surrounding rocky outcroppings. Its roots are so much
stronger and more prevalent than what is visible above the surface that they can
clump sand together, and keep it moist enough, to create an almost swampy, muddy
environment - at least by the rest of the deserts' standards.

It stands to reason its nutritional content would be very mineral-rich and
nutrient-poor, helping support
[lithoteric](/wildlife/fauna/defensive/tortolith.md) life that is not also
lithovoric.
