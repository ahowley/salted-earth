## Overview

A winged bat/bird-like creature with feathered wings and fleshy bodies, coming
in many shades but often a pale pink/red, white (albino?), and a pale gray. A
body the size of most harmoni, with double the wingspan.

## Diet

- [People](../../../people/people.md)
- Any flesh that cooks

## Lifespan

- 6 years

## Notes

You might think I'm making up scary tales to make this place sound worse than it
is, but Glassblowers are, in fact, flying, fire-breathing creatures that cook
their prey alive, indiscriminately. They have no known family or communal
behaviors and seem endlessly hungry.

Local biologists (or equivalent) seem to believe that there is no biological
cause for their size, meaning they could grow indefinitely if provided more
food. Folktales tell of Glassblowers the size of dunes from
[the Dune Sea](/world/regions/the-dune-sea/the-dune-sea.md), although that may
be overblown.

Interestingly, they seem equally able to _shrink_ - fortunately, not acutely,
but over time, they can reduce their bone and body structure to adapt to a lack
of food.

Their bones are lithoteric, hollow, and crystalline, being valued extremely
highly due to their rarity. They are almost exclusively harvested from those who
die of natural causes or territorial skirmishes, however, since hunting, taming,
or riding them is seen as a fools errand (indeed, many local euphemisms and
idioms derive from this fact - i.e. sarcastically, "when the Glassblower steak
is finished"). Some expert hunters do target them, however, although they're
employed more as a sort of renowned bounty hunter than one hunting for
sustenance, and only when one of these creatures is particularly troublesome.

Many creatures hold some spiritual significance to the people here, even those
who would otherwise ease survival challenges, such as the
[Icepetal](/wildlife/fauna/peaceful/icepetal.md). Glassblowers are certainly an
exception - each one that falls yields a celebration.
